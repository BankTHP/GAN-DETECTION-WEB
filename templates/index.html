The development of artificial intelligence technology is accelerating. This has led to the development of deepfake technology, which can create synthetic media to disguise a person's voice and face. Even a non-technical layperson can easily identify the program used for fake image synthesis with simply a large database of forgery targets. Therefore, face detection algorithms are a suitable technological advancement for the present.

THE DEEP FAKES ARE COMING

BUFFALO, N.Y. (WKBW) — Deepfake photo and videos can pose as a real threat to our ever-growing digital world and deep face expert Siwei Lyu believes these fake images will look even more real in the future.


“And if someone can manipulate that type of perception it can potentially become very, very dangerous," he said.

Deepfake images are digitally generated images or videos made with artificial intelligence. It can create high quality images of people who don't even exist. Or create videos of public figures doing or saying things they never did.

more video

We recognize the significance and are motivated to develop a model that can identify and differentiate between authentic and fake images using several GAN Models (Generative Adversarial Networks) and assess the effectiveness and precision of these models. Using the model to prevent attacks on the Face Recognition System and the use of manufactured photographs to produce fake news.
